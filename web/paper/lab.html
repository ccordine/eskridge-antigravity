Flight Guide

Resonator Rig

Tune the spring/cavity resonance, Q-loss profile, and pump feed into the oscillator.

In this sim, Tune omega0 lines the resonator up with the drive, Q reduces loss and stores oscillatory energy longer, and Pump beta pushes energy into the cavity faster. Best sequence: tune lock first, then raise Q, then raise beta and amplitude. Speed still comes from tilt once weight is unloaded.

If power drops while changing Q, check Osc Mag, k, and Lock. Lower pump draw can be good when the resonator is storing energy more efficiently; it is bad when lock or oscillator magnitude collapses.

  1. Launch: choose warp drive, ship, planet, and map; then start the session.
  2. Unweight: raise amplitude and adjust phase until Weight xg approaches hover or goes negative.
  3. Tune: use Recover Lock first. Keep Lock high and Phase Error small before pushing power.
  4. Store energy: raise Q and watch Osc Mag and k. If those collapse, back down and recover lock.
  5. Feed the cavity: raise Pump beta after lock is stable. Watch Drive, Osc Mag, and k together.
  6. Fly: use yaw for heading and tilt to convert lift authority into lateral fall.
  7. Assist: use Hover, Normal g, Ascend, and Descend for baseline trim; use Capture to bring targets to the current state.
  8. Unlocked sliders: when a vector control lock is off, that slider becomes a live indicator and follows the sim.
